Festivals of Gujarat

Experience the vibrant celebrations that bring communities together throughout the year, each festival reflecting Gujarat's rich cultural tapestry and spiritual heritage.

Major Celebrations

Join the festivities that define Gujarat's cultural calendar

Navratri

9 Days September/October
Garba Dance

Nine nights of devotion dedicated to Goddess Durga, featuring traditional Garba and Dandiya dances in colorful attire.

Uttarayan

2 Days January 14-15
Kite Flying

International Kite Festival marking the transition of sun into Capricorn, filling the sky with colorful kites.

Diwali

5 Days October/November
Rangoli & Diyas

Festival of lights celebrating the victory of light over darkness, with oil lamps, fireworks, and sweets.

Janmashtami

1-2 Days August/September
Human Pyramid

Celebration of Lord Krishna's birth with devotional songs, dances, and the famous human pyramid tradition.

Rann Utsav

3 Months November-February
Desert Culture

Cultural festival in the white salt desert of Kutch, showcasing local arts, crafts, music, and dance.

Sharad Purnima

1 Day October
Moonlight Celebrations

Harvest festival celebrated under the full moon with traditional dances and cultural performances.

Rath Yatra

1 Day June/July
Annual chariot

Annual chariot festival of Lord Jagannath in Ahmedabad, where beautifully decorated chariots are pulled through the streets with great devotion and celebration.

Holi

1 Day March
Festival of Colours

Festival of colors, celebrated with vibrant powders, music, and festive foods, symbolizing the arrival of spring.
